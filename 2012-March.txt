> > Of course, some bridges (or standards) supply the video data in a stupid

I don't think that'd be a problem per se, we could still make the USB
subsystem to put all the frame data into a virtually contiguous buffer,
regardles of how long the individual transfers are.

Bulk doesn't guarantee the length of the individual transfers either.

> > But those that deliver the whole frame in a Bulk transfer without

> 
> Not all the USB controllers support bulk transfers. It's a second
> choice, when possible, and, globally speaking, they are usually slower
> than the ISOC transfers.

Bulk has less overhead. Thus, if implemented well, it's faster. Isoc has
guaranteed throughput, and as such is a better match for streaming video
and audio.
