On Wed, May 24, 2006 at 12:56:25PM -0400, David Moore wrote:
> On Wed, 2006-05-24 at 16:29 +0200, Vojtech Pavlik wrote:
> 
> > So the question is: Is it possible to get the raw data out of the
> > QuickCam Pro 5000 CCD VGA sensor?
> 
> Getting a raw Bayer image is strangely not part of the UVC spec, and is
> thus not supported on the Quickcam Pro 5000 as best as I can tell.

I find that somewhat strange, too, but it's possible the authors of the
spec did want to hide/abstract the internal workings of the camera.

> It sounds like you may have used an undocumented feature to trick your
> iBot into giving raw output, and it's possible there's a similar hack
> for the Quickcam. 

It'd be nice if there was one. Most digital still cameras do have the
feature of obtaining the raw image - I'd expect it to be very useful for
webcams as well - more advanced filtering and interpolation can be done
by the main CPU than the builtin microprocessor.

When I set the camera to the 60Hz mode, I'm definitely operating it in a
mode the manufacturer didn't expect. The raw RGB mode is the native mode
of that camera, though.

Still, it's hard to call it an undocumented feature, because the
firmware in the iBot uP only can shuffle data from the video bus to USB,
and do I2C reads/writes to the sensor chip, nothing else.
