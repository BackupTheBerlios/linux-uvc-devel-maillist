Ajay Kumar Mishra wrote:
> uvccapture -t10 is supposed to grab the frames after every 10 seconds.
> Instead it just grab the frames at some predetermined rates. In fact, I
> changed the argument with option -t , but it did not change the rate of
> capture.

None of my other users have commented on the -t option incorrectly
timing captures. Iain Cuthbertson (iain at lazygnome.net) just checked this
for me using his caption.pl script, which adds a date and time to the
images. He says that the -t option acts as expected. If you're expecting
*exactly* 10 seconds, I can't guarantee that, I'm just using C timers
available in the standard library. If you have an underlying issue with
your time subsystem (as some Linux machines do) this could be a symptom.

Could you please download caption.pl from
http://www.lazygnome.net/projects/caption/0.1/caption-pl.txt
and use it to add the time/date stamps to your images to see what the
actual timing is? (please note it requires Image::Magick, use it with
uvccapture -c/path/to/caption.pl).
