Hi Philip,

Thanks for your comments. I did try submitting the jobs in batch modes to
make it simultaneous. But as frame grabbing happens in less than a second.
It does not make much of the difference.

Hence, I used POSIX threads to invoke three system commands simultaneously
in three different threads. All the threads begin at the same time. I have
verified in millisecond resolution and it seems to work perfectly well.

Let me also talk about another problem of grabbing the frames at regular
interval of <t> seconds. Here, I must confess that I was wrong about
uvccapture's -t option in my previous mail on the group. It does work. But
my requirement was capturing frames at 10FPS which could not be met as
uvccapture.c calculated time in seconds. Hence, I modified the uvccapture.c.
I am now reading reference_time and present_time using ftime() function. It
returns the time difference in milliseconds. And the rest remains the same.
With this modification, I am able to grab frames at 10 FPS.

thanks for valuable comments by Gabriel.

> > 2. Can the stream capture be parallelized for the three devices such
> > that they seem to capture the scene all at once?
>
> You could run three instances of uvccapture from the same script at
> roughly the same time by appending an & at the end of each line. They
> still won't run at *exactly* the same instance due to variable
> initialisation times for each camera and other environmental factors but
> it'll be closer than capturing from the three cameras sequentially.
>
> eg:
>
> uvccapture -d /dev/video0 &
> uvccapture -d /dev/video1 &
> uvccapture -d /dev/video2 &
